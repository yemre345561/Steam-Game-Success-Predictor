================================================================================
  7.1 — KEY RESEARCH FINDINGS (Summary of all chapters)
================================================================================

--- DATASET ---
- Source: Artermiloff 2025 Steam Dataset
- Raw records: 89,618 unique video game titles
- Raw features: 47 metadata columns
- Columns removed during preprocessing: 29 out of 47
- Final engineered feature matrix: 62 structural features
- Target variable: estimated_owners (lower bound extraction)
- Target structure: 3-class (Flop / Niche Hit / Successful)
- Original 4-class structure abandoned because "Mega Hit" tier had only
  186 games (0.21%), causing model collapse (19% F1-score)

--- CLASS DISTRIBUTION (Severe Imbalance) ---
- Flop (<20K owners): 67,738 games → 75.59%
- Niche Hit (20K-200K owners): 17,240 games → 19.23%
- Successful (>200K owners): 4,640 games → 5.18%
- A naive model predicting "Flop" for everything = 75.59% accuracy with
  ZERO predictive utility

--- DATA PREPROCESSING DECISIONS ---
- score_rank: 99.96% missing (89,579 nulls) → removed
- External critic URLs: 96.04% absent → removed
- Sentinel values (-1) in review columns: 40.64% of rows (36,419 records)
  → corrected to 0, binary flag (has_reviews) engineered
- Developer names: 19,460 unique values → removed to prevent overfitting
- Publisher names: 18,837 unique values → removed to prevent overfitting
- Post-launch metrics removed: pct_pos_total, num_reviews_total, peak_ccu
- release_year removed: temporal metadata, not developer-controllable
- has_reviews removed: indirect proxy for post-launch popularity

--- FEATURE ENGINEERING ---
- Numerical features retained unscaled (tree-based models don't need normalization)
- Binary flags created: is_free, has_achievements, has_dlc, is_early_access,
  has_multiplayer, has_coop, has_controller, has_trading_cards, has_workshop,
  has_website, supports_mac, supports_linux
- Language support: 8 languages one-hot encoded (Chinese, Japanese, German,
  French, Russian, Korean, Brazilian PT, Spanish)
- Genres: 10 genres one-hot encoded
- Tags: Top 25 tags selected by highest divergence between Flop and Successful
  tiers, then one-hot encoded
- screenshot_count and required_age retained as numerical features
- Total: 62 features, all pre-launch and developer-controllable

--- MODEL ARCHITECTURE ---
- Algorithm: Random Forest Classifier (ensemble of decision trees)
- Baseline comparison: Single Decision Tree → 68.87% accuracy
- Random Forest → 77.44% accuracy (8.57 percentage point improvement)
- Hyperparameters (optimized via GridSearchCV):
  * n_estimators: 300
  * max_depth: 25
  * min_samples_split: 10
  * min_samples_leaf: 4
  * max_features: sqrt
  * class_weight: balanced (to counteract class imbalance)
  * random_state: 42

--- DATA LEAKAGE EVIDENCE ---
- Experiment 3 (with post-launch review metrics):
  * Accuracy: 86.22%
  * Macro-F1: 78.04%
- Experiment 4 (structural features only — FINAL MODEL):
  * Accuracy: 77.44%
  * Macro-F1: 63.48%
- Drop: 14.56 percentage points in Macro-F1
- This proves review counts act as PROXIES for sales, not genuine predictors
- has_reviews appeared in 97% of Successful games but only 51% of Flops
  → classic data leakage indicator

--- PER-CLASS PERFORMANCE (Final Model - Experiment 4) ---
- Flop:     Precision=89.3%, Recall=85.3%, F1=87.2%
- Niche Hit: Precision=45.7%, Recall=51.3%, F1=48.3%
- Successful: Precision=50.2%, Recall=60.6%, F1=54.9%
- Overall Accuracy: 77.44%
- Macro-F1: 63.48%

--- CONFUSION MATRIX (Test Set: 17,924 games) ---
- Flop (actual 13,548):     11,550 correct | 1,659 → Niche | 339 → Successful
- Niche (actual 3,448):     1,299 → Flop | 1,768 correct | 381 → Successful
- Successful (actual 928):  181 → Flop | 185 → Niche | 562 correct

--- CROSS-VALIDATION ---
- Method: 5-Fold Stratified Cross-Validation
- Mean Macro-F1: 63.52%
- Standard Deviation: ±0.32%
- This minimal variance proves the model captures genuine structural patterns,
  not artifacts of a favorable random train-test split

--- LEARNING CURVE ---
- Training and cross-validation scores converge as training size increases
- Convergence plateau at approximately 30,000 training instances
- Narrowing gap between curves = evidence of generalization, not overfitting

================================================================================
  7.2 — CONTRIBUTIONS (Practical + Theoretical)
================================================================================

--- PRACTICAL CONTRIBUTIONS ---
1. Deployable Predictive System: A functional Random Forest model that classifies
   games into 3 commercial tiers using only pre-launch structural attributes.
   This is NOT a post-hoc analysis — it is forward-looking and actionable.

2. Quantified Feature Prioritization: Exact percentage weights for each of the
   62 features, enabling developers to make mathematically justified decisions
   about feature investment (e.g., "adding 5 more languages increases my
   probability of moving to a higher tier").

3. Data-Driven Product Roadmap: The feature importance hierarchy directly
   translates into a sequential development checklist:
   Phase 1: Tag optimization + storefront visuals
   Phase 2: Pricing + content depth (achievements, DLC, trading cards)
   Phase 3: Social mechanics + platform accessibility

4. Localization Priority Sequence: Empirically validated language prioritization
   (English → Chinese → German → French → Russian → Korean) for
   resource-constrained indie studios.

5. Web Application Prototype: A Flask-based predictor app allowing real-time
   success predictions for new game concepts.

--- THEORETICAL CONTRIBUTIONS ---
1. Resource-Based View Validation: Successfully predicting commercial outcomes
   using ONLY internal, firm-controllable attributes validates that internal
   resource allocation (not just external market forces or publisher brand)
   drives competitive advantage in digital entertainment markets.

2. Data Leakage Methodology Case Study: The documented 14.56pp Macro-F1 drop
   when removing post-launch metrics provides a transferable methodological
   lesson for applied ML research in entertainment, e-commerce, and other
   domains where post-outcome contamination is a risk.

3. Crowdsourced Metadata as Predictive Signal: The finding that community-driven
   tags (31.1%) rival traditional numerical attributes (33.1%) contributes
   empirical evidence to the literature on platform economies and algorithmic
   curation (tags are created by users, not developers).

4. Structural Ceiling Quantification: The 63.48% Macro-F1 score defines the
   empirical "glass ceiling" of what pre-launch structural data can predict.
   Everything beyond this requires post-launch signals (gameplay quality,
   viral dynamics, community reception) that are inherently unquantifiable
   pre-release.

================================================================================
  7.3 — LIMITATIONS AND FUTURE RESEARCH
================================================================================

--- LIMITATION 1: TARGET VARIABLE PROXY ---
- estimated_owners is a STATISTICAL APPROXIMATION, not verified sales data
- Valve does not publicly release actual sales figures
- The metric is community-sourced (originally SteamSpy methodology)
- Introduces measurement noise into classification boundaries
- However: it is the most widely used proxy in ALL game analytics literature

--- LIMITATION 2: STRUCTURAL FEATURE CEILING ---
- Macro-F1 of 63.48% represents the maximum achievable with structural data alone
- The model's biggest weakness: Niche Hit classification (F1=48.3%)
- Mid-tier games lack definitive structural identity — their attributes overlap
  with both Flops and Successful titles
- Factors that determine Niche → Successful transition are UNQUANTIFIABLE:
  * Subjective gameplay quality / "fun factor"
  * Viral community moments (streamer attention, social media trends)
  * Influencer endorsements and YouTube/Twitch coverage
  * Unpredictable cultural timing
  * Word-of-mouth cascade effects
- These represent the "irreducible error" boundary of any pre-launch model

--- LIMITATION 3: STATIC TEMPORAL SNAPSHOT ---
- Dataset captures a SINGLE cross-sectional snapshot (early 2025)
- Steam's recommendation algorithms are continuously updated
- Consumer preferences and market trends evolve rapidly
- Genre popularity cycles change over time
- The structural relationships identified may shift in future market conditions

--- LIMITATION 4: CLASS IMBALANCE ---
- 75.59% Flop dominance creates inherent prediction bias
- Despite class_weight='balanced', the model still struggles with minority tiers
- The 4-class structure completely collapsed for Mega Hits (186 games, 19% F1)
- Extremely rare success events are statistically impossible to generalize from

--- LIMITATION 5: EXCLUDED VARIABLES ---
- Developer/publisher identity deliberately removed (19,460 / 18,837 unique)
- This prevents overfitting but also removes potentially valid "brand equity"
  and "track record" signals
- Unstructured text data (descriptions, summaries) excluded — would require NLP
- Visual assets (screenshots, trailers) excluded — would require computer vision
- External marketing data (social media spend, ad campaigns) not available

--- LIMITATION 6: GEOGRAPHIC AND CULTURAL BIAS ---
- The dataset is predominantly Western-market oriented
- Asian markets (China, Japan, Korea) may have different success dynamics
- Cultural factors in game design preferences are not captured
- Platform-specific behaviors on different storefronts (Epic, GOG) not included

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 1: NLP SENTIMENT INTEGRATION ---
- Incorporate user review text analysis via Natural Language Processing
- Hybrid model: structural features + early post-launch sentiment scores
- Chi-Square feature selection on review text
- Could capture qualitative "fun factor" signals the current model misses

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 2: TEMPORAL / SEQUENTIAL MODELING ---
- Deploy time-series or recurrent neural networks (LSTM/GRU)
- Model the EVOLUTION of success metrics over time, not just a snapshot
- Capture seasonality effects (holiday sales, summer releases)
- Track how algorithmic recommendation changes affect game visibility over time

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 3: DEEP LEARNING ON VISUAL DATA ---
- Use Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) on capsule images, screenshots,
  and promotional artwork
- Visual quality of store page directly impacts consumer purchase psychology
- Could quantify the "aesthetic appeal" dimension that storefront presentation
  research has identified as critical

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 4: CAUSAL INFERENCE ---
- Transition from CORRELATIONAL feature importance to CAUSAL modeling
- Use Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs) or instrumental variables
- Current model shows "tags correlate with success" but cannot prove
  "more tags CAUSE success"
- Causal framework would strengthen strategic recommendations

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 5: REAL-TIME PROSPECTIVE VALIDATION ---
- Deploy the model as a live web application
- Track predictions for NEWLY released games and compare to actual outcomes
- Prospective validation = ultimate test of generalizability
- Would also enable continuous model retraining with fresh data

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 6: MULTI-PLATFORM COMPARISON ---
- Extend analysis to Epic Games Store, GOG, PlayStation Store, Xbox Marketplace
- Compare whether the same structural features predict success across platforms
- Would test whether findings are Steam-specific or universal to digital distribution

--- FUTURE RESEARCH DIRECTION 7: ADVANCED ENSEMBLE / BOOSTING ---
- Test Gradient Boosting (XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost) against Random Forest
- Literature suggests LightGBM may outperform RF on tabular game data
- Could potentially push the structural ceiling beyond 63.48% Macro-F1
