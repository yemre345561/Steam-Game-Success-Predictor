APPENDIX B: HYPERPARAMETER OPTIMIZATION GRID

	The Random Forest classifier was tuned using a grid search with cross-validation to maximize its ability to generalize to unseen data. The optimization targeted four key hyperparameters: the number of trees (n_estimators), maximum tree depth (max_depth), minimum samples required to split (min_samples_split), and minimum samples at a leaf node (min_samples_leaf).

	We tested 54 candidate configurations using three-fold stratified cross-validation. The Macro F1-score was used as the target metric to account for class imbalance, ensuring the model did not simply favor the majority "Flop" class. Table B.1 displays the tested parameters and the final selections. Notably, the final values for n_estimators (300) and max_depth (25) were manually refined after the initial grid search to balance computational limits and avoid overfitting. Other baseline parameters, such as the balanced class weight, were fixed to penalize minority class errors proportionally.

APPENDIX C: TAG SELECTION DIVERGENCE ANALYSIS

	Steam allows free-form user tags, resulting in a vocabulary too large for a standard tree-based classifier. We applied a divergence-based selection method to identify the 25 most critical gameplay tags and avoid excessive dimensionality.

	This approach compared tag frequencies between the two extreme performance tiers in our dataset: Successful games (over 200,000 owners) and Flops (under 20,000 owners). The Niche Hit tier was excluded to highlight the sharpest contrast. For each candidate tag, we calculated the absolute percentage-point difference between its presence in the Successful tier and the Flop tier.

	As shown in Table C.1, the Multiplayer tag had the highest positive divergence, appearing in 41.9% of Successful titles but only 5.7% of Flops (a 36.1 percentage point gap). Conversely, five tags appeared more frequently in the Flop category. The Casual tag was the most notable negative indicator, appearing in nearly a third of all Flop titles. All 25 retained tags demonstrated sufficient divergence to serve as meaningful split criteria for the model.
